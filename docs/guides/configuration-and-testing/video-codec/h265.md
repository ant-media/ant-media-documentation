# H265 (HEVC)

H265 (HEVC - High Efficiency Video Coding) provides better video quality at lower bitrates compared to H264, making it ideal for bandwidth-sensitive streaming scenarios.

---

## Why Use H265?

- **Higher Compression Efficiency:** Delivers similar quality at lower bitrates compared to H264  
- **Bandwidth Optimization:** Ideal for high-resolution (HD/4K) streaming  
- **Better Video Quality:** Improved visual quality at reduced data usage  

---

## Limitations

- **Limited Browser Support:** Most browsers do not support H265 playback in WebRTC  
- **Licensing Requirements:** Unlike VP8, H265 is not royalty-free  
- **Playback Constraints:** Requires compatible players or devices  
- **Not Ideal for WebRTC Alone:** Needs fallback (H264) for browser compatibility

H265 is disabled by default. You can enable it from the Application Settings (from version 3.0.1 onward) or via Advanced Settings in earlier versions.

### Enable from Application Settings
- Navigate to **Application Settings**
- Enable **H265 codec**
- Save the configuration

## Streaming and Playback with H265

### Send H265 Stream

- Use a tool like OBS to publish an RTMP stream with the H265 codec  
- Refer to the Enhanced RTMP guide for configuration details:  
👉 https://antmedia.io/docs/guides/publish-live-stream/rtmp/enhanced-rtmp/

---

### Playback Support

- **Android Devices:** Native support via HLS, LL-HLS, and DASH  
- **Desktop Players:** VLC and other third-party players support H265 playback  
- **Browsers:** Most browsers do not support H265 playback in WebRTC  

Check browser compatibility here:  
👉 https://caniuse.com/?search=H.265

---

### Play H265 Transcoded Streams with H264

Since H265 support is limited in browsers and WebRTC, enable H264 alongside H265 and use adaptive bitrate (ABR) streaming:

```js
"h264Enabled": true,
"h265Enabled": true,
```

:::info
At least one adaptive bitrate (ABR) must be enabled. Without ABR, Ant Media Server will not transcode H265 streams into H264, and playback will fail on browsers that do not support H265.
:::
